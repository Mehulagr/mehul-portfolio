    Designing for Loan Officers
    Ellie Mae


    Duration: Aug'17 - Present


    Overview: Ellie Mae is an enterprise mortgage software company. The company is working to automate and streamline the mortgage process from initial application to closing and selling of the loan in the secondary market. The software helps lenders lower the loan origination cost by automating services and significantly reducing the time to close the loan. Currently, ~40% of the mortgages in the United States pass through the software. Until recently, Ellie Mae was majorly a desktop client and is now moving its entire platform to the web, thus creating a web/mobile application for its users.


    As a UX designer at Ellie Mae, my role is focussed on designing experience for Ellie Mae’s Encompass platform (both web and mobile). I am also working with the design system team to unify, design and spec various web components for different Ellie Mae products.


    LOAN OFFICER: Going Mobile

    Loan officers are the primary users of Ellie Mae’s flagship software, Encompass. Not only are they involved in each step of the loan process from loan origination to selling the loan, but their work also requires them to be on the go most of the time. Sometimes they visit borrowers homes, or at cafes to collect required documents and/or walk them through the terms of the loan. They sometimes set up shop at the realtors' office where they can build a relationship with the realtors and get referrals. The mobility of the loan officers means that they use other tools or hacks to supplement data into the encompass software, which has traditionally been a windows only application. Thus, the product team decided to make a major enhancement to the software by making it mobile and giving loan officers access to some major feature set wherever they are, whenever they want.


    Since mobile was a new domain for Ellie Mae, we had to start with basics to understand what was important to the loan officers when they are on the go. I was involved right from the beginning of the project from user research to working with the developers during implementation. Some of my contributions to the project lifecycle are-

    • A quick and dirty prototype for research
    • Shadowed the UX researchers during the research and testing sessions
    • Translated existing web architecture to the mobile context and re-designed the navigation patterns for the existing mobile app.
    • Handed-off final UX requirements to visual designers in the form of high-fidelity mockups and specs.
    • Worked with devs to get the right experience implemented.

    Design System

    Along with working on web and mobile platform for the encompass software, I have also been part of the design system team. During UI sanity checks, we noticed a lot of inconsistencies in implementation due to the fact that certain components were hard-coded. They looked and ofter worked differently. Also, there was a lack of standard visual language within the design team which resulted in redundancy of effort. Thus, setting up a design system became the core of Ellie Mae’s transformation from the desktop client to the web platform.

    For designing components, we started looking at existing components and are trying to unify and make their definitions consistent across the board. I have been talking to designers and product manager to understand the different use case for a component, particularly for their product.
