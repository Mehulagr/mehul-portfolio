    Designing for Loan Officers
    Ellie Mae


    Duration: Aug'17 - Present


    Overview: Ellie Mae is an enterprise mortgage software company. The company is trying to automate and streamline the mortgage process from initial application to closing and selling of the loan in secondary market. The software helps lenders lower the cost of loan by providing various services and significantly reduce the time to close the loan. Currently, 40% of the mortgages in United states pass through the software. Until recently, Ellie Mae was majorly a desktop client and is now moving its entire platform to web, thus creating a web/mobile application for its users.


    As a UX designer at Ellie Mae my role is focussed on designing experience for Ellie Mae’s Encompass platform (both web and mobile). I am also working with the design system team to unify, design and spec various web components for different Ellie Mae products.


    LOAN OFFICER: Going Mobile

    Subtitle

    Loan Officers are really dynamic users. They spend most of their time outside the office. Their job demands them to work from different locations such as coffee shop, realtors office, borrowers house, etc. They also visit borrowers home or meet them at a coffee house to collect documents/ explain them terms. They also setup temporary office at realtors to build relationship with them and to work with them to help borrowers.

    When on the go, a lot of loan officers carry a tablet or laptop so that they can view/ edit information. Sometimes they end up mailing their assistant to edit or view simple information. Other times they have make shift apps to access information. Thus, it was important to provide them a mobile solution.

    I worked with BA and PM to design quick and dirty wireframes which was then used for researching needs of the user by UX researcher. Combining the data from the research and product teams understanding, we were able to prioritize the features for the users.

    Designing for power users

    Subtitle

    Usually desktop client such as Ellie Mae’s Encompass provide a lot of flexibility and power to users like Underwriters who thrive on this flexibility and power. Some of the tasks that they perform are- matching value from a document to values in the loan file to make sure all numbers are correct, moving around files and pages to make sure that all the documents are present and in right order.

    Bringing all those options on web is a challenging task, both from dev and design perspective. Thus, for this task I closely collaborated with the business analyst and researcher to understand the different use cases. I have actively been working with team to make sure users can achieve the same tasks that they did on desktop client but more easily.
